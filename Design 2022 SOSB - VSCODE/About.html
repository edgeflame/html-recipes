Why I have chosen this problem and how does it affect children eating in the cafeteria?

This product has been made because recently the school cafeterias have been focusing on
only Indian and very seldomly on Italian food. I wish to eliminate this by providing a more
balanced diet with different cultural varieties.

To do this, I've researched about various recipes and their nutritional information, taste, cost
and how effecient they would be in replacing the current school cafeteria system. However,
Indian food will will still be served due to the familiary we possess regarding our culture, and
the convenience of making it with already present ingredients.

The recipes I have shortlisted are from Greek, Japenese and Vietnamese. This is because
the source (healthline) considers them one of the top healthy cuisines among their 10
shortlisted cuisines. To know why, keep reading below.

GREEK CUISINE

One of the most
researched diets is the traditional Greek diet. It places a strong emphasis on grains, beans,
vegetables, fish, and unsaturated fats. As a result of its well-known ingredients, chickpeas,
peppers, and sardines, this food has exceptionally high nutritional qualities, especially in
omega-3. The surprising lack of meat in it further lowers the intake of saturated fats. Given
that "salad" and "yoghurt" are typically used after the word "Greek," it shouldn't come as a
huge surprise that this national cuisine is one of the healthiest in the entire globe.

JAPANESE CUISINE

Balance, diversity, and moderation are the three cornerstones of healthy eating, and
the Japanese diet serves as an excellent example of each. People frequently live to be
over 100 years old in some regions of Japan, such as Okinawa, and their nutrition has
a significant impact on this. Foods like yams, green tea, seaweed, soy products, and
shiitake mushrooms also have a number of health benefits, including being high in
iron, calcium, zinc, copper, omega-3 fatty acids, and antioxidants. The diet is abundant
in fruits and vegetables, which have been shown to have cancer-fighting properties.
Many of their foods are steamed or quickly fried, and many of them practise the art of
Hara Hachi Bu, or eating until you're 80% full, at the dining table. These eating habits
only increase the health benefits of their diet.

THAILAND CUISINE

Thai cuisine is one of the world's healthiest because it is bursting with potent flavours and frequently contains a lot of veggies. Turmeric, coriander, ginger, lemongrass, and chilli peppers are a few of the spices and flavours that distinguish Thai cuisine. Curcumin, a substance found in turmeric in particular, has been intensively researched for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant qualities (14Trusted Source). Additionally, garlic and onions, which both have anti-cancer qualities and have been shown in test-tube tests to help inhibit the growth of cancer cells, are frequently used in Thai cuisine
